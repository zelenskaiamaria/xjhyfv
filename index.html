Nebulae are huge clouds made up of dust and gas. Their properties include absorption and emission of light. Nebula formation occurs due to the effect of gravity on cosmic dust and interstellar gas.

Nebulae were originally defined as any fixed luminous astronomical objects, such as star clusters or galaxies beyond the Milky Way, which could not be divided into stars. Galaxies outside the Milky Way that could not be divided into stars. Some examples of this usage have survived to this day. For example, the Andromeda galaxy is sometimes referred to as the Andromeda Nebula.
